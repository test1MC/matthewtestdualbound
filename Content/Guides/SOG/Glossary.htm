Glossary

Term

Definition

Account Executive/ Account Manager

Surescripts personnel responsible for managing the relationship between Surescripts and its various customer and partner channels including pharmacy chains, PBM/payers, and vendors.

Accreditation

The process in which certification of competency, authority, or credibility is presented.

Case

An incident becomes a case once reported to Surescripts Support. Case number, type, and severity will be assigned internally. Case is an interchangeable term for “trouble ticket” or “work ticket” which includes problems, information and change requests.

Certificate Authority

Certificate Authority (CA). An organization that issues digital certificates to customers for the purpose of authentication and encryption.

Certification

The confirmation through external review and audit of certain characteristics of an organization, business division or product/service.

Customer

Those that access and send transactions over the Surescripts network –providers, pharmacies, payers – and the vendors that connect them.

Customer Support (Level 1 Support)

Surescripts Level 1 Support (Customer Support). Customer Support researches, analyzes, triages issues, and escalates issues to Level 2 Support, as needed.

Customer Production Support (Level 2 Support)

Surescripts Level 2 Support (Customer Production Support) addresses issues that may require external coordination, detailed research, lengthy periods of time to resolve, and/or a deep systemic understanding. Level 2 Support may receive cases escalated by Level 1 Customer Support after research, analysis, and triage, as needed.

EHNAC

Electronic Healthcare Network Accreditation Commission (EHNAC) is an independent, federally recognized, standards development organization and non-profit accrediting body designed to improve transactional quality, operational efficiency and data security in healthcare.

ePA

Electronic Prior Authorization (ePA) is the process that requires specific authorization from a “Payer” (health plan, processor, or PBM) to dispense a medication based on a number of factors, such as medical necessity, prior treatment options, and clinical qualifiers.

E-Prescribing

Service that allows eligible new prescriptions to be sent directly to the computer at a pharmacy of the patient’s choice. Renewal authorization requests can be sent directly to the prescriber’s computer. Providers can then review and respond to pending requests with a few keystrokes.

Escalation

Surescripts determines the escalation and assignment of each case to ensure efficient resolution.

Formulary/Eligibility

Message type that gives providers the ability to request and receive eligibility and formulary information from payers and PBMs.

HIPAA

HIPAA is the federal Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996. The primary goal of the law is to make it easier for people to keep health insurance, protect the confidentiality and security of healthcare information and help the healthcare industry control administrative costs.

HISP

Health Information Services Provider. An organization that provides security and transport services for directed exchange based on the Direct protocol.

Incident

An incident is defined as any event that is not part of the standard operation of services and which causes or may cause an interruption in the quality of service.

Integration Project Manager (IPM)

A Surescripts manager responsible for providing education, consultation and project management for network customers implementing Surescripts products. Integration project managers maintain a deep understanding of the Surescripts internal development processes and products to deliver quality, in-depth and direct implementation support.

ISO 27001

An international specification for information security management. The corresponding code of practice is ISO/IEC 27002.

ITIL

(ITIL Continual Service Improvement) (ITIL Service Design) A set of best-practice publications for IT service management. Owned by the Cabinet Office (part of British HM Government), ITIL gives guidance on the provision of quality IT services and the processes, functions and other capabilities needed to support them. The ITIL framework is based on a service lifecycle and consists of five lifecycle stages (service strategy, service design, service transition, service operation and continual service improvement), each of which has its own supporting publication.

Message Type

Individual messages exchanged through the Surescripts network that are part of larger services (Benefit, History, Routing, Clinical Message, ePA, Medication Management, etc.).

Organization

Organizations represent routable entities on the Surescripts network for messages destined to or from an entity that is not identified as a specific individual. Organizations are broken down into organization types to further define the entity. Current organization types are Pharmacy, General, and Payer. New organization types should be expected to be added in the future.

Payer

An organization that holds data and manages pharmacy benefits for a population of members. Includes PBMs and health plans. See PBM.

PBM

Pharmacy Benefits Manager. See Payer.

Pharmacies

"Pharmacy” means any place where Drugs are Dispensed and Pharmacist Care Services are provided.

 

PHI

Protected Health Information. Shall have the same meaning as defined in 45 CFR Parts 160 and 164. Examples of PHI include, but are not limited to: names, telephone numbers, fax numbers, electronic mail addresses, social security numbers, medical record numbers, health plan beneficiary numbers, account numbers, and any other unique identifying number, characteristic, or code.

Prescription Benefit

Service that allows easy selection of medications that are preferred by a patient’s health plan, meet therapeutic guidelines and are cost-effective for the patient. Message types included in this service: Formulary/Eligibility and Reporting.

Production

A live environment where customers are sending and receiving transactions on the Surescripts network. No testing in production is allowed for E-Prescribing. PHI data may only be sent in the production environment; it is not allowed in any of the other environments.

Providers

Collective term for physicians, nurse practitioners and physician associated staff.

Registration Authority

Registration Authority (RA). An organization that is responsible for collecting and verifying information about customers requesting a certificate before telling a CA to issue the certificate.

Reporting

Message type that provides utilization summaries for the prescription benefit service.

Resolution

The term used to identify the solution to the issue or question posed in a support case. The resolution should provide a root cause and/or solution to the issue or an answer to the question. Surescripts Support staff communicates this resolution to the customer.

Response

A response is defined as a communication regarding updates to a support case or more information becomes available.

Security

Security management as defined by the ISO 27001, and ENHAC standards.

Service Availability

Availability = [(Total Time – Unplanned Downtime) /Total Time]

Availability is the long-term average fraction of time that the specified Surescripts product is in service and satisfactorily performing its intended function.

Service Availability (Availability Management)

(ITIL Service Design) The process responsible for ensuring that IT services meet the current and future availability needs of the business in a cost-effective and timely manner. Availability management defines, analyses, plans, measures, and improves all aspects of the availability of IT services, and ensures that all IT infrastructures, processes, tools, roles, etc. are appropriate for the agreed service level targets for availability.

Severity

Severity indicates the level of impact an issue (or case) may have to the customer’s business. Severity levels are assigned internally and determine the targeted resolution time.

Software

Specific product or technology that a vendor provides.

Staging

A test environment (also known as the certification or customer environment) where customers can test their transactions prior to going live in production. PHI data is not allowed in the staging environment.

Surescripts Severity Level 2

Issue causes major or moderate impact to customer’s business (e.g. systemic/consistent errors, staging environment communications/network issues).

Surescripts Severity Level 3

Information inquiries or issues causing minor impact (e.g. directory update requests, eligibility questions, and missing scripts).

Third-party Groups

Third-party groups will include, but are not limited to: EHNAC, Direct Trust, FBCA and Federal Bridge guidelines, SOC-2, HIPAA, HITRUST and NIST.

Vendor

A technology company that sells to network customers the software solutions that have been certified by Surescripts to connect to the Surescripts network.
