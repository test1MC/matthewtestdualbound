In addition to this guide there is a guide for acute settings, which is was created to assist hospitals in developing and transferring messages needed to provide physicians treating hospitalized patients access to outpatient medication history.

The guide lists data variances PBMs will see when medication history requests originate from a hospital. that explains how to check eligibility. Please contact your account representative to obtain a copy of the X12-NCPDP Medication History Acute and Prescription Benefit implementation guides.

Prescription Benefit Implementation Guide

The Surescripts Prescription Benefit Implementation Guide was created to assist Pharmacy Benefit Managers (PBMs) and Prescriber Systems in developing and transferring messages needed to provide PBM member data (eligibility information, pharmacy benefit coverage, and group-specific formulary information) to physicians in an ambulatory setting.

Directories Implementation Guide

The Surescripts Directories Guide was created to assist pharmacies and Prescriber Systems in developing and transferring directory related messages containing Surescripts related and demographic data for a pharmacy and prescriber. Complete and accurate directories are essential for prescription routing. This document represents XML and EDIFACT implementations.

Medication History – Acute Implementation Guide

The Surescripts Medication History – Acute guide was created to assist hospital systems in developing and transferring messages needed to provide physicians treating hospitalized patients access to outpatient medication history and assist pharmaceutical benefits management (PBM) companies as a claims source in responding to a medication request and developing a medication response.

Prescription Routing Implementation Guide

The Surescripts Prescription Routing Guide was created to assist pharmacies and prescriber vendors in developing and transferring messages needed to provide direct conveyance of new prescriptions from physician to pharmacy, and of refills and change requests from pharmacy to physician.
