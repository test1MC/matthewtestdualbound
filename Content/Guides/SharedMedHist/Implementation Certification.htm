Implementation & Certification

Implementation, Certification, and Production

Implementation Process

You will be invited to a Surescripts education session, receive Surescripts network guides and requirement documentation. Your account will be set up to access the Surescripts staging/certification environment. The timeframe of the Implementation Phase can vary depending on your resource allocation for the project.

Certification Process

This phase includes more detailed testing of the transactions and the user interface to ensure that all Surescripts requirements are met. You will be assigned a Certification Project Manager who will work with you through the completion of certification testing. Surescripts will provide more detail surrounding the milestones that are necessary to certify and move into production. Once a participant passes certification, services are turned on in production.

Transition to Production

Once Certification is complete, the participant is ready to move into production. Surescripts will schedule a hand-off meeting for the business and technical staff of both Surescripts and the participant to discuss the following:

  • Production Support Contacts (Surescripts’ and participant’s)
  • Support Process
  • Support Hours

Certification Requirements

The Surescripts certification process ensures that participant software is able to send and receive electronic messages in accordance with industry standards and that it provides open choice for medication selection and dispensing location. In addition, Surescripts certification focuses on patient safety, efficiency of the electronic prescribing process and ease of use by end-users.

Surescripts certification testing focuses on message format and application workflow, including display, per the Surescripts Implementation Guides and application requirements within those guides. By holding all participants equally accountable for meeting the certification application requirements, our participants can send and receive the highest quality electronic transactions as e-prescribing continues to progress overall as an industry.

The application requirements that participants must meet in order to be certified on the Surescripts network are defined throughout this guide. These requirements are denoted with an asterisk following the word “shall*” and a unique identifier for tracking purposes. These requirements will be enforced as part of certification. Requirements that are enforced as part of the production code are simply denoted as “must”. All of these requirements shall be met in order to successfully complete certification. “Should” is used for guidance or what was previously called best practices. See the following chart for terminology usage.

Term

The application requirements participants are required to meet in order to be certified on the Surescripts network. These requirements will be enforced as part of certification.

Medication History Prescriber Vendor Requirements

  1. The application shall*106M-011 allow the end user to change a patient's consent to obtain medication history between, at a minimum, the yes and no consent values.
  2. The application shall*106M-012 display a disclaimer regarding use of medication history retrieved via Surescripts.
    1. Sample Disclaimer: Certain information may not be available or accurate in this report, including items that the patient asked not be disclosed due to patient privacy concerns, over-the-counter medications, low cost prescriptions, prescriptions paid for by the patient or non-participating sources, or errors in insurance claims information. The provider should independently verify medication history with the patient.
  3. When a data element from the medication history response is displayed, the application shall*106M-014 have the ability to display all data received for that data element.
  4. If the application displays medication history from sources other than Surescripts, the application shall*106M-020 identify which medication history was retrieved via Surescripts.
  5. The application shall*106M-018 have the ability to display all medication history Drug/MedicationDispensed loops retrieved, unless prohibited by federal, state or local laws.
  6. The application shall*106M-019 provide access to all history data available for a specific request. When a medication history response indicates more medication history is available, the application shall*106M-026 either systematically retrieve all medication history available or notify the end user more medication history is available.
  7. For PBM/Payer claim data, the participant shall*106M-016 retrieve medication history for eligible coverages only.

    8. For pharmacy fill data, the participant may retrieve medication history regardless of patient eligibility.

  8. For PBM/Payer claim data, the participant shall*106M-017 retrieve medication history for all eligible coverages.
  9. The claim medication history request for a patient shall*106M-013 be tied to the most recent eligibility response for that patient. This is accomplished by using the ISA control number (ISA-13) from the 271 Eligibility response in the UIB-030-02 <RelatesToMessageID> of the RXHREQ Medication History Request.
