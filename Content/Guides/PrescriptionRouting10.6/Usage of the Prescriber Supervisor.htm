Usage of the Prescriber/Supervisor/Agent

The following examples show the looping of the PVD Segment when reporting different physician entities. "Designated Agent" refers to the person without an identifier submitting the prescription on behalf of a prescriber (for example, an administrative assistant, or another prescriber that is covering for a prescriber on vacation). Supervisor information may be needed based upon state law. A supervisor is the supervising physician under whose authority the prescription is being prescribed by the primary care provider.

Denoting Only the Prescriber

EDIFACT Example:

PVD+PC+1234567890:HPI+++JONES:MARK'

Segment

Value

Note

PVD-010

PC

Identifies this PVD segment as the information about a prescriber (PC = Primary Care Physician).

PVD-020

1234567890:HPI

The NPI of the prescriber.

PVD-050

JONES:MARK

The PC's name: Mark Jones.

XML Example:

  <Prescriber>

    <Identification>

      <NPI>1234567890</NPI>

    </Identification>

    <Name>

      <LastName>JONES</LastName>

      <FirstName>MARK</FirstName>

    </Name>

Prescriber and Designated Agent

EDIFACT Example:

PVD+PC+1234567890:HPI+++JONES:MARK+++++JACKSON'

Segment

Value

Note

PVD-010

PC

Identifies this PVD segment as the information about a prescriber (PC = Primary Care Physician).

PVD-020

1234567890:HPI

The NPI of the prescriber. HPI is the qualifier for NPI.

PVD-050

JONES:MARK

The PC's name: Mark Jones.

PVD-100

JACKSON

The Designated Agent's last name.

XML Example:

  <Prescriber>

    <Identification>

      <NPI>1234567890</NPI>

    </Identification>

    <Name>

      <LastName>JONES</LastName>

      <FirstName>MARK</FirstName>

    </Name>

    <PrescriberAgent>

      <LastName>JACKSON</LastName>

    </PrescriberAgent>
