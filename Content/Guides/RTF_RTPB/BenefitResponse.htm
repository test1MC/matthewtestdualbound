This field qualifies the amount in the Maximum Amount.

Values:

TimePeriod

 RTF

Type of time period associated with the overall Maximum Amount Qualifier.

Values:

CM = Calendar Month – Specifically, the time elapsed from the start of the first day of a month until the end of the last day of that same month; frequently accepted as a period beginning with a specific event on the Nth day of a month and ending at the end of the day prior to the Nth day of the next month.

CQ = Calendar Quarter – Specifically, the time elapsed from the start of the first day of a month until the end of the last day of the second month that follows; traditionally these periods start on the first day of the months of January, April, July and October.

CY = Calendar Year – Specifically, the time elapsed from the start of the first day of a year until the end of the last day of that same year; frequently accepted as a period beginning with a specific event on the Nth day of a year and ending at the end of the day prior to the Nth day of the next year.

DY = Days – Units of time composed of 24 consecutive hours.

LT = Lifetime – An imprecise time reference that equates to the perceived time remaining until either the end of the patient’s life or the useful duration of a product referenced.

PD = Per Dispensing – An imprecise time reference that equates to the perceived time between dispensing events.

SP = Specific Date Range – A specific period of time, as qualified by start and end dates.

C AlternativeMedicationInformation Both

Clinically appropriate alternatives provided here.

For RTF workflow, alternatives are provided only when a single medication is sent in the Benefit Request, they are not returned for multiple medications requests.

Possible Pharmacy Type Combinations – RTPB Workflow

Potential Retail and Mail Combinations

  • retail only
  • mail only
  • retail, 90 day retail
  • retail, mail
  • retail, 90 day retail, mail
  • mail at 30 days, mail at 90 days

The same Pharmacy Type combinations that exist on the Medication Requested may also exist on Alternative Medications.

Possible Specialty Responses

The specialty Pharmacy Type may apply to a retail or mail pharmacy. Use the NCPDP ID under the Pharmacy Type as an identifier.

Specialty Responses are applicable only to a specific list of specialty medications, not all. For a specialty medication request:

  • If the requested pharmacy is mail, then the PBM may switch the requested mail NCPDP ID to a specialty mail NCPDP ID;
  • If the requested pharmacy is retail and the medication is available at the requested retail, then the PBM will show retail pricing and may also show pricing at specialty mail;
  • If the requested pharmacy is retail and the medication is not available at that retail, then the PBM may show specialty mail;
  • If the requested pharmacy is specialty and a 90 day supply is also available on an appropriate 30 day medication request, then the PBM may return both the 90 and 30 day supply options.

Notes on Pricing – RTPB Workflow

  • Retail pricing is only provided at the retail pharmacy included in the original Benefit Request. Additional retail pharmacy options are not provided in the Benefit Response.
  • If a mail order NCPDP ID is sent, then only mail order pricing and coverage information will be returned.
