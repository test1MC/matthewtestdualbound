Coverage Alert Reference Codes and Text

A Coverage Alerts summary is shown below:

Coverage Alert Definition Examples Displaying Alerts
AgeLimits Age constraints apply to the coverage of the specified NDC. Retin-A gel is only covered for patients under the age of 22. A prescriber submits a PMBC Benefit Request (RTPB workflow) for Retin-A for a patient who is 45 years old and a coverage alert is provided. Display this alert and the related Reference Text which displays the specific age limit.
DaysSupplyExceeded Days supply requested exceeds plan limitation. A prescriber submits a PMBCBenefit Request (RTPB workflow) for a medication to a retail pharmacy with a Days Supply of 90. The patient's benefit only allows for a 30 Days Supply at retail. A coverage alert is provided notifying that the Days Supply is exceeded. Display this alert and the related Reference Text which provides the Days Supply allowed.
StepTherapy Certain medications are not covered unless members have tried one or more "prerequisite therapy" medication(s) first. PMBC Benefit Request (RTPB workflow) is for Altoprev. The patient's benefit only allows coverage for Altoprev if the patient has tried a generic therapeutic alternative first. If there is no generic therapeutic alternative in the patient's medication claims history, a coverage alert is provided notifying that Step Therapy is required. Display this alert and the related Reference Text which may include prerequisite medication options.
NotOnFormulary Medications or products that are not included on a list of preferred products that a committee of medical reviewers has determined to be the safest and most cost effective. These products may be not covered or are covered at a higher out of pocket cost for a patient. PMBC Benefit Request (RTPB workflow) is submitted for brand name Lipitor. The patient's benefit covers the generic, Atorvastatin, only. A coverage alert is provided notifying that Lipitor is a Non-Formulary medication and therefore Not Covered. Display this alert.
PharmacyOutOf Network Requested pharmacy is not contracted with the patient's prescription benefit plan. Patient may incur higher out of pocket costs. PMBC Benefit Request (RTPB workflow) is submitted for a medication to be filled at a retail pharmacy. The pharmacy is not included in the patient's pharmacy network coverage and a coverage alert is provided notifying that the requested pharmacy is out of network. Display this alert.
GenderLimits Medication coverage is restricted to a specific gender. PMBC Benefit Request (RTPB workflow) is submitted for Premarin for a male patient. The patient's benefit only covers Premarin for females and a coverage alert is provided notifying that Gender Limits apply. Display this alert.
QuantityLimits or QuantitiyLimit Exceeded Quantity requested is outside of plan limits. PMBC Benefit Request (RTPB workflow) is submitted for Imitrex 100 mg tablets with a Quantity of 20 and Days Supply of 30. The patient's benefit only allows 12 tablets every 30 days. A coverage alert is provided notifying that quantity limits are exceeded. Display this alert and the related Reference Text which provides limitation information.
PlanLimitExclusion PMBC Benefit Request is outside of plan limits. Possible examples include: quantity limits exceeded, days supply exceeded, days supply exceeded at requested pharmacy, age limits exceeded, maximum dose exceeded. PMBC Benefit Request (RTPB workflow) is submitted for Levothyroxine 0.125 mg for a quantity of 30 with a 1 day supply. The maximum daily dose is exceeded for this medication and a Coverage Alert is provided notifying that plan limits are exceeded. Display this alert and the related reference text which provides limitation information.
ExcessiveUtilization Maximum daily dose may be exceeded based upon the Quantity and Days Supply submitted; or patient may have a remaining quantity of medication from a previous fill. PMBC Benefit Request (RTPB workflow) is submitted for Atorvastatin 10 mg tablets for a Quantity of 90 with a 90 Days Supply. Patient filled a prescription for this Quantity and Days Supply of the same medication 30 days previously. Since patient should have an existing quantity on hand, the benefit does not allow the claim to adjudicate. A Coverage Alert is provided notifying of Excessive Utilization. Display this alert and the related reference text which provides limitation information.
TextMessage     Display this data. Text messages are typically provided as supplemental information to coverage alerts.
