Product Overview

About Insights for Medication Adherence

Surescripts Insights for Medication Adherence provides actionable data at the point of care to increase adherence and improve patient outcomes while reducing costs by putting patient-specific adherence summaries and real-time messages from PBM/payers in the provider vendor workflow. With this actionable intelligence, issues and gaps in care can be identified quickly with medication adherence. Then the caregivers can address those problems during and in-between patient visits, in partnership with PBM/payers and provider vendors.

Treatment Plan Adherence

Insights for Medication Adherence provides a Proportion of Days Covered (PDC) score that allows providers to quickly see if patients are taking their medications as prescribed.

PDC is calculated by PBM/payers based on the refill records obtained from prescription claims, and shows the percentage of time patients have medications available to take. 

Currently Medication adherence summaries are provided for hyperlipidemia, hypertension, and diabetes.

Identifying and Closing Gaps in Care

Insights for Medication Adherence also provides medication-specific messages based on the patient’s diagnosis and PDC score so you can easily identify and address gaps in care. Three types of messages are provided:

  • High risk medications: medications that could cause adverse effects
  • Medication adherence: medications not being properly taken
  • Missing medications: drugs that should be taken, but weren’t prescribed

Providing (Optional) Feedback to PBM/Payers

Insights for Medication Adherence clinicians have the option to provide feedback to PBM/payers based on conversations with patients. This will help tailor the medication messaging received from PBM/payers in the future.

Prerequisites for Insights for Medication Adherence

When there is a patient encounter, the provider vendor should run an eligibility request/response (X12 270 and 271 messages) to obtain the Payer Identification and the PBM Unique ID needed to identify the patient. If there is no patient encounter, the demographic MPI search is used. See section Demographic MPI Search for more information.
