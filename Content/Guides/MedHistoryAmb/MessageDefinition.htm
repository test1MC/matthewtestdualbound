C

Date Composite

If empty, up to the last 50 medications filled are returned, starting with the most recent.

If populated with an effective and expiration date, up to 50 medications are returned to the requester, starting with the expiration date.

When more medications are available, a More Drugs Available (AQ) response is returned in the ApprovalReasonCode and then another RxHistoryRequest is needed to obtain additional available medication history.

M

  EffectiveDate

EffectiveDate is the beginning date for the desired history (e.g., 1 year ago from today’s date).

Up to one year of medication history fill data can be requested.

It is suggested that no more than one year of medication history claim data be requested.

If the Date composite is used, both the effective date and expiration date are required.

M

  ExpirationDate

ExpirationDate is the end date for the desired history (e.g., today’s date).

This should be set to the date the RxHistoryRequest was initiated for the initial request, if populated. This would be set to the last medication’s LastFillDate in the previous RxHistoryResponse if the "AQ" indicator was in the ApprovalReasonCode.

Up to one year of medication history fill data can be requested.

It is suggested that no more than one year of medication history claim data be requested.

If the Date composite is used, both the effective date and expiration date are required.

Structured SIG XML Requirements

Structured SIG is conditional. The intent is not to facilitate the reconstruction of the SIG to human readable form (English), but rather to communicate through electronic means the SIG components in a controlled, well-defined structure. It is to be used to relay information between the prescriber and the pharmacist. It is not patient instructions. 

SIG data will only be sent in Directions for fill data.
