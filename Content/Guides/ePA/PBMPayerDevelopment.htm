PBM/Payer Development and Integration

Drug and Patient Matching

The PAInitiationRequest might not include the PBM Member ID in all cases. PBM/payers must develop secondary patient-matching criteria to successfully find the patient. Unmatchable patients may go into to a manual queue, or the PAInitiationRequest could respond with automated “patient not found”.

Work Queue

There are several instances where prior authorizations might need to be manually worked:

  • Drug cannot be matched
  • Provider vendor sends an attachment
  • Denials and other cases where you are contractually or regulatory obliged to send to a manual queue

Exceptions and Medicaid Process

PBM/payers should consider having a generic question set for all Medicaid prior authorizations since these are typically not medication/condition specific.

Workflow and Internal Process

PBM/payers should consider sending coded references (e.g. LOINC codes) along with questions in a question set. This will allow the provider vendor to extract information from the patient's record instead of manually answering each question.

Handling Prior Authorization Priority

Priorities and processing requirements vary among health plans and other government programs, so the NCPDP standard does not support a priority flag. Rather, in cases where PBM/payers have to determine the priority of a PA, a question should be provided to determine the priority of the request within the question set.

Service Date

As there is no discrete ServiceDate field in the PAInitiationRequest, PBM/payers should treat the PAInitiationRequest message SentTime field as the de facto service date of the message.
