Support

Surescripts Support focuses on ensuring that customers have access to the appropriate support services to ensure high quality connectivity, communications, and technical operations using the Surescripts network.

Surescripts Support primary functions are to:

  • Manage technical relationship with customers
  • Serve as point of contact for any technical issues
  • Track and escalate issues and work with Surescripts and customer technical staff to resolve issues
  • Manage Surescripts services

Surescripts Customer Support Responsibilities

Surescripts Support Teams

Level 1 Support - Customer Support

  • Initial point of contact for all logged case intake and initial triage.
  • Communications to all external parties including those pertaining to:

    • Outages (internal & external)

    • Maintenance window and maintenance window changes
    • Bulletins (status updates)
  • Administrative support on customer interface tools

Level 2 Support – Customer Production Support:

  • Contact for issue escalations requiring external coordination, detailed research, longer periods of time to resolve and/or a deep systemic understanding; including:

    • Internal support of systemic issues
    • External communications of issues discovered by Surescripts Systems Monitoring and Operations
    • Escalated request handling

Level 2 – Customer Production Support may send after-hours communications and notifications.

Surescripts Business Hours Definitions

Surescripts Support Business Hours

Normal business hours for Surescripts are 8:00 AM to 8:00 PM ET, Monday through Friday, excluding Surescripts' holidays. Surescripts will send a Support Notification email to connected customers to notify them of a Surescripts observed holiday one week before the holiday.

Surescripts Support – After Hours

After-hours support is defined as all day Saturday and Sunday, all Surescripts holidays, and between the hours of 8:00 PM and 8:00 AM ET, Monday through Friday. After-hours, support coverage is provided for Sev 1 issues on an on-call basis.

Surescripts Service Level Definitions

Sev 1: Issue causes severe impact to customer’s business or a Potential Patient Safety Issue (PPSI), (e.g. no workaround is immediately available, mission critical data is lost or corrupted, production network outage, misrouted message to unintended recipient).

Sev 2: Issue causes major or moderate impact to customer’s business
(e.g. operations can continue in a restricted fashion, severely degraded performance, a temporary workaround is available, systemic/consistent errors, staging environment communications/network issues).

Sev 3: Informational inquiries or issues causing minor impact (e.g. error message with workaround, minimal performance degradation, incorrect product behavior with minor impact, directory update requests, eligibility questions, file processing questions).

Surescripts Potential Patient Safety Issue (PPSI) Definition

A PPSI is a Severity 1 support issue that must be reported to Surescripts immediately upon discovery. It is important to understand and distinguish the potential impact of a PPSI (one which affects the completeness/accuracy of a transaction) from that of reliability, connectivity, and throughput issues (those that impact the delivery, delay, non-delivery, or the perception of a missing transaction).

Surescripts PPSI Examples

Examples may include the following:

  • Missing, truncated, garbled and/or modified information
  • Improper mapping of data
  • Improper display of information
  • Incorrect routing

Customer Production Support Responsibilities

Customer Technical Support

Customer Technical Support is defined as the first line of issue notification, enrollment/directory process and workflow, and case management support provided to the end users. Customer Technical Support staff shall provide first-line technical support at their end user site(s) for the following:

  • Routine questions and issues related to the Surescripts systems or network as it relates to the Surescripts products and services for which the customer is certified;
  • Routine questions and issues related to the customer’s software application, system or network;
  • Routine questions and issues, as applicable, to other aggregate network or technology vendor applications or support protocols should the customer connect using such third party;
  • Determination of issue source;
  • Issue logging and tracking;
  • Securing resolution information requirements;
  • Issue escalation, as needed.

Customer Support Alias Contact

As part of the Surescripts Support Escalation and Case Management processes, each customer account must provide a single distribution-style support alias email address. Customer is responsible for managing those team members in its account who need to be auto-copied on all on-going correspondence, as well as the process of additions, deletions, and/or modifications to any of their team member emails. If there are additional escalation points of contact for a customer’s account that are not included in the Support alias address, customers may forward these names and email addresses to Surescripts Customer Support for inclusion on notification emails as appropriate/requested.

Whenever possible, the Support alias email address used should be descriptive enough of customer account for Surescripts Customer Support to distinguish which customer account in question owns the communication address. Surescripts does not accept any “generic” or public/network domain email alias (e.g. AOL, Gmail, Yahoo) for use of support-related processes or issues. Any of the following alias email addresses could be used and are acceptable:

  • Support@CustomerDomainName.com,
  • CustomerName@CustomerDomainName.com,
  • CustomerNameSupport@CustomerDomainName.com.

It is the responsibility of the customer’s account to notify Surescripts of any updates/modifications to customer’s technical support contacts as soon as possible.

Customer Support Availability

The customer technical support team must be able to respond to an escalated inquiry within one business day or less (depending on issue severity) during normal business hours. On-call technical support staff must be available during non-business hours. In cases of vacation or excess case volume, customer will designate a back-up resource and communicate holiday schedules to Surescripts Support.

Customer Training

Customer is responsible for end-user training on the appropriate application workflows for its product or service. Customer is also responsible for providing the first line technical/directory support to the end users on network-related issues.

Customer’s technical and enrollment support team members should register for and participate in training (offered by Surescripts or customer vendor) to understand the basics of any Surescripts product or service for which customer’s software is used on the Surescripts production network.

Initial “Go-Live” training to support teams is required before access to the Surescripts production support tools is provided. An overview of initial training provided to vendor customers is provided post certification and assignment to an account. Surescripts Production Support will provide supplemental support-related training opportunities on a variety of relevant topics and tools.

Customer Case Creation and Submission

Customers who connect directly to the Surescripts network must follow the recommended support protocol as described within this document. Customers connecting through a gateway aggregator/network should provide, as outlined, specific information when reporting information to their aggregator who will in turn report the issues to Surescripts for assistance. As appropriate, all incidents should be researched on the respective customer network/connections and in the Admin Console prior to logging a case with Surescripts Customer Support.

Surescripts Case Management is handled by one of several related tools in Salesforce:

  • Self-Service Portal (SSP)
  • Partner Portal
  • Salesforce-to-Salesforce

Surescripts will provide customer with credentials to access the appropriate tool(s) once enabled in the production environment. For purposes of this document, the SSP will be used as the default tool for description purposes. Minor or nominal issues must be reported via the SSP and will automatically be assigned a case number and routed to Surescripts Customer Support. Non-critical issues that are reported via email or telephone may be delayed by up to two business days in processing.

Surescripts strongly recommends use of the SSP for high-priority Severity 1 issues as it generates the appropriate internal notifications and escalations to after-hours on-call Customer Production Support. Customers may report high priority issues via email or telephone, but should be mindful of the times these communication venues are monitored; emails or voice mail messages received after posted business hours may not be reviewed until the following business day. Issues should not be emailed to individual Surescripts Customer Support or Customer Production Support team members.

To promote timely review/assistance in the case management process, Surescripts requests that the customer provide details and insight into the issue and, depending on the type of issue (transaction, directory, administrative, etc.), provide critical messaging or record details, such as:

  • Description of issue (e.g. missing script, failed message send, failed message receipt, failed file load, faxed refill, vendor switch, failed web service request, etc.)
  • Time/date message or file was sent/received, if applicable
  • Sender Message ID or Transaction #, if applicable
  • Message Type
  • Pharmacy NCPDP ID or Store Name/Address, if applicable
  • Provider SPI or First/Last Name and Address, if applicable
  • Qualifying details or remarks from the end user(s)
  • Organization or provider credentials/demographics (e.g. NPI, DEA, Address), if applicable

!! IMPORTANT !!

Protected Health Information (PHI) should NEVER be submitted in the free-text areas of a case logged to or in email correspondence with Surescripts. PHI data may only be provided via secure email.

Once a case has been established, customers should use the case comments section of the case management tool to move forward/monitor the support process. When it is necessary to send information on a case via email, customers should always copy support@surescripts.com and include the case number. Upon creation and/or resolution of a case, Surescripts generates an email acknowledgment to Customer Technical Support. All other case email notifications will be provided to the customer team member email address provided in the case.

The timeframe for an acknowledgment of receipt of the case and initial review by Surescripts Customer Support/Customer Production Support will be based upon the case severity as defined by the Surescripts Support Quick Reference Card.

Customer Issue Escalation/Critical Incident

Customers will identify an internal process for assigning and managing all cases received from Surescripts. For Severity 1, high priority and PPSI cases, the customer will identify an internal workflow that will provide Surescripts with timely and detailed research/resolution information on the issue. As requested, customer management/executive escalation must be available to discuss Severity 1 issues and assist in expedited resolution. Issues identified that put the patient community at risk (PPSI) may require suspension of active service levels/records. Customers will provide Surescripts with a root cause analysis for all Severity 1 cases within 24 hours of issue resolution.

Please see Glossary for issues that are identified as “Critical Incidents”.
