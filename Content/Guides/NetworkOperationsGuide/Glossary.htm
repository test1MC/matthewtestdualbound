Glossary

Term

Definition

Account Executive/ Account Manager

Surescripts personnel responsible for managing the relationship between Surescripts and its various customer and partner channels including pharmacy chains, PBM/payers, and vendors.

Accreditation

The process in which certification of competency, authority, or credibility is presented.

Acknowledgement

The notification by Surescripts to the originating Surescripts’ customer of our receipt of an incident and creation of a formal ‘case’.

Case

An incident becomes a case once reported to Surescripts Support. Case number, type, and severity will be assigned internally. Case is an interchangeable term for “trouble ticket” or “work ticket” which includes problems, information and change requests.

Certificate Authority

Certificate Authority (CA). An organization that issues digital certificates to customers for the purpose of authentication and encryption.

Certification

The confirmation through external review and audit of certain characteristics of an organization, business division or product/service.

Change Management

(ITIL Service Transition) The process responsible for controlling the life-cycle of all changes, enabling beneficial changes to be made with minimum disruption to IT services.

Clinical Direct Messaging

Clinical Direct Messaging connects healthcare providers nationwide via the Surescripts network and offers convenient, secure delivery of clinical communications and data for improved care coordination and increased workflow efficiency.

Critical Incident/ Severity Level 1

All critical incidents are Severity Level 1; however, not all Severity Level 1 cases are defined as Critical Incidents based on breadth and source/cause of the critical incident issue.

A potentially high profile event or recurring complex event resulting in an unexpected or undesirable result which, if not handled in an appropriate and timely manner, may cause severe negative impact to Surescripts, Surescripts stakeholders, Surescripts clients, patients or the reputation of e-prescribing.

A critical incident is an event that threatens the company either financially, operationally, legally or in regard to reputation and warrants immediate attention. A critical incident is usually systemic, multifunctional, multi-departmental, and has a longer life cycle. Most critical incidents can be broken into the following categories; however, not all events within the following categories are critical incidents and not all critical incidents will fall into these categories:

-Patient Safety Incidents

-Security Incidents

-Operational Incidents

Customer

Those that access and send transactions over the Surescripts network: providers, pharmacies, payers – and the vendors that connect them.

Customer Support (Level 1 Support)

Surescripts Level 1 Support (Customer Support). Customer Support researches, analyzes, triages issues, and escalates issues to Level 2 Support, as needed.

Customer Production Support (Level 2 Support)

Surescripts Level 2 Support (Customer Production Support) addresses issues that may require external coordination, detailed research, lengthy periods of time to resolve, and/or a deep systemic understanding. Level 2 Support may receive cases escalated by Level 1 Customer Support after research, analysis, and triage, as needed.

EHNAC

Electronic Healthcare Network Accreditation Commission (EHNAC) is an independent, federally recognized, standards development organization and non-profit accrediting body designed to improve transactional quality, operational efficiency and data security in healthcare.
Electronic Prior Auhorization Electronic Prior Authorization (ePA) is the process that requires specific authorization from a “Payer” (health plan, processor, or PBM) to dispense a medication based on a number of factors, such as medical necessity, prior treatment options, and clinical qualifiers.

E-prescribing

E-prescribing is “the transmission", using electronic media, of prescription and prescription-related information between a prescriber, dispenser, pharmacy benefit manager, or health plan, either directly or through an intermediary, including an e-prescribing network. E-prescribing includes, but is not limited to, two-way transmissions between the point of care and the dispenser. The term electronic prescriptions would not apply to prescriptions communicated by facsimile (fax) or over the phone.

Escalation

Surescripts determines the escalation and assignment of each case to ensure efficient resolution.

Formulary/Eligibility

Message type that gives providers the ability to request and receive eligibility and formulary information from payers and PBMs.

HIPAA

HIPAA is the federal Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996. The primary goal of the law is to make it easier for people to keep health insurance, protect the confidentiality and security of healthcare information and help the healthcare industry control administrative costs.

HISP

Health Information Services Provider. An organization that provides security and transport services for directed exchange based on the Direct protocol.

Incident

An incident is defined as any event that is not part of the standard operation of services and which causes or may cause an interruption in the quality of service.
Insights for Medication Adherence Insights for Medication Adherence managed and interactive medication management information to providers, during and in-between patient visits, in partnership with PBM/payer and provider vendor partners.

Integration Project Manager

A Surescripts manager responsible for providing education, consultation and project management for network customers implementing Surescripts products. Integration project managers maintain a deep understanding of the Surescripts internal development processes and products to deliver quality, in-depth and direct implementation support.

ITIL

A set of best-practice publications for IT service management. Owned by AXELOS, ITIL gives guidance on the provision of quality IT services and the processes, functions and other capabilities needed to support them. The ITIL framework is based on a service life-cycle and consists of five life-cycle stages (service strategy, service design, service transition, service operation and continual service improvement), each of which has its own supporting publication.

IT Service Management

The implementation and management of quality IT services that meet the needs of the business. IT Service Management is performed by IT service providers through an appropriate mix of people, process and information technology.

Master Patient Index (MPI)

The implementation and management of quality IT services that meet the needs of the business. IT Service Management is performed by IT service providers through an appropriate mix of people, process and information technology.

Medication History

Service that allows providers to access critical information on their patients’ current and past medications from Pharmacy Benefit Managers and pharmacies.

Message Type

Individual messages exchanged through the Surescripts network that are part of larger services (Benefit, History, Routing, Clinical Message, ePA, Medication Management, etc.).

Payer

An organization that holds data and manages pharmacy benefits for a population of members. Includes PBMs and health plans. See PBM.

PBM

Pharmacy Benefits Manager. See Payer.

Pharmacy

Any place where Drugs are Dispensed and Pharmacist Care Services are provided.

PHI

Protected Health Information. Shall have the same meaning as defined in 45 CFR Parts 160 and 164. Examples of PHI include, but are not limited to: names, telephone numbers, fax numbers, electronic mail addresses, social security numbers, medical record numbers, health plan beneficiary numbers, account numbers, and any other unique identifying number, characteristic, or code.

Potential Patient Safety Issue (PPSI)

An issue that impacts the completeness/accuracy of a transaction that could result in harm to a patient.

Prescription Benefit

Service that allows easy selection of medications that are preferred by a patient’s health plan, meet therapeutic guidelines and are cost-effective for the patient. Message types included in this service: Formulary/Eligibility and Reporting.

Production

A live environment where customers are sending and receiving transactions on the Surescripts network. No testing in production is allowed for e-prescribing. PHI data may only be sent in the production environment; it is not allowed in any of the other environments.

Providers

Collective term for physicians, nurse practitioners and physician associated staff.

Real-Time Prescription Benefit

Real-Time Prescription Benefit delivers patient-specific medication cost and benefit information to the point of care to help inform providers’ prescription decisions.
Record Locator & Exchange Powered by Surescripts' nationwide network, Record Locator & Exchange is a query and retrieve service that gives providers a fast and easy way to obtain historical patient visit locations and retrieve clinical records, regardless of geography or EHR system.

Registration Authority

Registration Authority (RA). An organization that is responsible for collecting and verifying information about customers requesting a certificate before telling a CA to issue the certificate.

Reporting

Message type that provides utilization summaries for the prescription benefit service.

Resolution

The term used to identify the solution to the issue or question posed in a support case. The resolution should provide a root cause and/or solution to the issue or an answer to the question. Surescripts Support staff communicates this resolution to the customer.

Response

A response is defined as a communication regarding updates to a support case or more information becomes available.

Security

Security management as defined by HITRUST and EHNAC standards.

Service Availability

Availability = [(Total Time – Unplanned Downtime) /Total Time]

Availability is the long-term average fraction of time that the specified Surescripts product is in service and satisfactorily performing its intended function.

Service Availability (Availability Management)

(ITIL Service Design) The process responsible for ensuring that IT services meet the current and future availability needs of the business in a cost-effective and timely manner. Availability management defines, analyzes, plans, measures, and improves all aspects of the availability of IT services, and ensures that all IT infrastructures, processes, tools, roles, etc. are appropriate for the agreed service level targets for availability.

Service Performance (Performance Management)

Activities to ensure that something achieves its expected outcomes in an efficient and consistent manner.

Severity

Severity indicates the level of impact an issue (or case) may have to the customer’s business. Severity levels are assigned internally and determine the targeted resolution time. See Surescripts Severity Levels 1 - 3, below.

Software

Specific product or technology that a vendor provides.

SPI

Surescripts Provider ID.

SSP

Surescripts Self-Service Portal.

Staging

A test environment (also known as the certification or customer environment) where customers can test their transactions prior to going live in production. PHI data is not allowed in the staging environment.

Surescripts Severity Level 1

Issue causes severe impact to customer’s business or is a Potential Patient Safety Issue (e.g. production network outage, prescription message routed to wrong customer).

Surescripts Severity Level 2

Issue causes major or moderate impact to customer’s business (e.g. systemic/consistent errors, staging environment communications/network issues).

Surescripts Severity Level 3

Information inquiries or issues causing minor impact (e.g. directory update requests, eligibility questions, missing scripts).

Surescripts Support Levels

Support Level 1 – Customer Support, which researches, analyzes, triages issues, and escalates issues to Level 2 Support, as needed.

Support Level 2 – Customer Production Support

Subject Matter Expert (SME) Escalation Resources – Account Executive/Account manager or Integration Project Manager or other – escalated as appropriate.

Surescripts Support Quick Reference Card

The Surescripts Support Quick Reference Card is a quick reference to Surescripts support information, including the response/resolution times based on severities, and is available on the Self-Service Portal.

Third-party Groups

Third-party groups will include, but are not limited to: EHNAC, Direct Trust, FBCA and Federal Bridge guidelines, SOC-2, HIPAA, and NIST.

Vendor

A technology company that sells to network customers the software solutions that have been certified by Surescripts to connect to the Surescripts network.
