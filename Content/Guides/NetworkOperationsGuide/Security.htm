Customer Security Responsibilities

Customers are responsible for the development and maintenance of security technologies, procedures and policies at a maturity sufficient to ensure that their network connection to Surescripts:

  • Meets security standards outlined in product implementation guides;
  • Is configured securely;
  • Is used only for authorized purposes;
  • Maintains a security patch management program; and
  • Protects against improper access, use, loss, alteration, or destruction of business records and transmission of data.

Identity Proofing

Identity Proofing for e-Prescribing Use Only

Surescripts has a number of access management policies and procedures in place to help maintain network security. Surescripts has a policy to meet the Identity Proofing (IDP) standards developed by the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST), requiring all new prescribers who use e-prescribing software undergo IDP to connect to the network. IDP is critically important for the secure electronic exchange of health information.

Surescripts has developed IDP requirements for all prescriber customers connecting to the Surescripts network for e-prescribing transactions. These requirements are captured in the document Surescripts Identity Proofing Requirements. Customers implement the IDP requirements tier that best meets their business model. The implemented IDP process must be approved by Surescripts before it can be released into production.

Identity Proofing for Clinical Messaging Use Only

The Surescripts organization operates as the Registration Authority (RA) and Certificate Authority (CA) on behalf of its customers as part of the HISP services offered within the Clinical Messaging product.

Surescripts is required to comply with all DirectTrust policies in order to remain a member in good standing and to maintain RA and CA EHNAC accreditations. Currently, DirectTrust requires that all organizations and/or individuals that access the DirectTrust network be issued a credential only after undergoing identity verification according to NIST Level of Assurance (LoA) - 3. As such, Surescripts requires that each end-user organization agree and ensure this procedure is being performed.

To comply with these requirements, Surescripts validates the identity of a representative from each organization connecting to the Clinical Messaging network using the identity proofing (IDP) process. The Surescripts IDP Process meets the DirectTrust policies by following the remote verification methods for LoA-3, outlined within the NIST Special Publication 800-63-2: Electronic Authentication Guideline.

Identity Proofing For Electronic Prior Authorization® QuickStart™

Surescripts Prior Authorization Portal® QuickStart™ is a free web site that providers can use to process prior authorizations. To ensure that Surescripts appropriately protects PHI, all users must enter a business associate agreement with Surescripts and those that wish to use Surescripts Prior Authorization Portal® QuickStart™ at the point of care must undergo ID Proofing, which consists of positive verification of identity.

Certificate Management Obligations

A customer that utilizes certificates to establish secure connections with Surescripts will:

  • Protect its Private Keys from compromise (including if employing a HISP who uses secure processes against potential compromise),
  • Provide accurate and complete information and communication to the Issuer CA and RA,
  • Confirm the accuracy of certificate data prior to using the certificate,
  • Promptly cease using a certificate and notify the Issuer CA if (i.) any information that was submitted to the Issuer CA or is included in a certificate, changes or becomes misleading or (ii.) there is any actual or suspected misuse or compromise of the Private Key associated with the certificate,
  • Use the certificate only for authorized and legal purposes, consistent with the relevant Certification Practice Statement (CPS) and Subscriber Agreement, (including only installing device certificates on servers accessible at the domain listed in the certificate), and
  • Promptly cease using the certificate and related Private Key after the certificate’s expiration.
  • Promptly notify the appropriate CA upon suspicion of loss or compromise of their private key(s). Such notification shall be made directly or indirectly through mechanisms consistent with the CA’s CPS.
