3

Eligibility

Retrieval

Removed 3.3: The participant shall request eligibility for a patient only once during a three (3) calendar day period unless an error is encountered in the eligibility retrieval process.

Removed 3.4: Information returned in the Eligibility response (e.g. Member

Unique ID) shall be used within three (3) calendar days of receipt.

Removed 3.5: PBM and the participant shall be able to process an eligibility request/response that has both the subscriber and dependent levels populated. 1 Process does not mean that the participant can systematically default to returning an error in this scenario.

Removed 3.7: Application shall alert the user if a given field in the application allow for more characters than the X12 standard transaction allows for that data element.

Removed 3.10: The application shall make the patient demographics returned in the 271 available for the prescriber to review.

Removed 3.12: The choice of which pharmacy to route the prescription to shall be based solely on the decision of the patient and prescriber. The participant shall not create a default, systematic link between an active eligibility coverage and a mail order pharmacy for prescription routing.

4

General Formulary and Benefits

Requirements

Removed 4.7, On the prescription writing screen the application shall display, at a minimum, formulary status, all copay data for all pharmacy types provided, and an indication that a coverage restriction applies without user action. Additional detailed coverage information shall be made available via a method of the participant's choice (for example, a new window from user click or hover).

Alternative Functionality: On the prescription writing screen the application shall display, at a minimum, formulary status, an indication that copay information is available, and an indication that a coverage restriction applies without user action (the latter two may use the same indicator). The detailed copay and coverage information shall be made available via a method of the participant's choice (for example, a new window from user click or hover).

Note: This alternative will limit access to certain PBM/Payer data.

Removed 4.11: The application shall display formulary status and abbreviated retail copay data for all alternatives without user action. Abbreviated retail copay data displayed shall be, at a minimum, the tier, flat amount or percent rate.

Alternative Functionality: The application shall display formulary status and an indication that copay information is available for all alternatives without

user action. The detailed copay information shall be made available via a

method of the participant's choice (for example, a new window from user click or hover).

Note: This alternative will limit access to certain PBM/Payer data.

Removed 4.12: The application shall order alternatives identified in a formulary alternatives list according to the stated preference level indicated in that list.

Removed 4.14: The application shall display all strengths and dosage forms of alternatives identified in a formulary alternatives list.

Note: Alternatives identified in a formulary alternatives list indicate drug at the drug name level only - not the specific drug name/strength/dosage form level implied by the NDC used to identify the alternative.

Removed 4.15: The application shall display all coverage information. Note: Multiple coverage records, across coverage types and/or within a coverage type, may exist and all shall be displayed.

Removed 4.16: The application shall display all copay information.

Note: The rule to determine which copay, drug specific or summary, applies to a drug is defined in the Surescripts Implementation Guide.
