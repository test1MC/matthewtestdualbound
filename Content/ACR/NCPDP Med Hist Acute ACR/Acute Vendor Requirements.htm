Acute Vendor Requirements

Numeric Representation

2.1 The decimal point is represented by a period and shall*106M-001 be used as follows:

  • only when there are significant digits to the right of the decimal
  • when there is a digit before and after the decimal point
  • not with whole numbers

For example, consider the following possible values:

Correct:

2.515

251.5

25.15

2515

0.2515

2.5

Incorrect:

.2515

2515.

3.00

Phone Number: Pharmacy, Prescriber and Patient

2.9 Phone Number shall*106M-025 be in the format 5552223333X4444 - where 555 is the area code, 2223333 is the main number and X4444 is the extension.

2.10 Phone number shall*106M-028 not be populated with all zeros or other default values. This field is 80 characters long to handle email addresses. (Please note the extension X4444 is just an example. Less/more than four digits are allowed).

XML

2.11 Most symbols should be ‘safe’ within most alphanumeric fields; however, XML validation has issue with the following characters and they shall*106M-035 be escaped. The most common escape encodings are:

ASCII Characters

XML Escape

quote ("")

&quot;

apostrophe (')

&apos;

ampersand (&)

&amp;

less than (<)

&lt;

greater than (>)

&gt;

String lengths are calculated based on the un-escaped value. For example, “>98 degrees” should be encoded as “&gt;98 degrees” but would only be counted as a string length of 11, not 14. The quote and apostrophe only need to be escaped if they are used within an attribute.

2.12 The application shall*106M-012 display a disclaimer regarding use of medication history retrieved via Surescripts.

  • Sample Disclaimer: Certain information may not be available or accurate in this report, including items that the patient asked not be disclosed due to patient privacy concerns, over-the-counter medications, low cost prescriptions, prescriptions paid for by the patient or non-participating sources, or errors in insurance claims information. The provider should independently verify medication history with the patient.

2.13 The application shall*106A-001 provide access to all history data available for a specific request. When a medication history response indicates more medication history is available, the application shall systematically retrieve all medication history available.

2.14 One instance of the provider identification/reference number <Prescriber><Identification> shall*106A-002 be the Participant ID of the participant making the medication history request.

This uses the <MutuallyDefined> element.
